Frequently asked Questions

What is the Chimera System?

The Chimera System is an electronic analog switching system for pickups.

What is the Chimera System for?

The Quimera System allows you to make various types of connections, such as series, parallel, series/parallel and parallel/series, between the individual pickups coils, allowing you to obtain tones from traditional guitars such as Stratocaster, Telecaster, Les Paul and several exclusive tones that they are not obtainable on common guitars.

What types of guitars can the Chimera System be installed on?

O Quimera System pode ser instalado em qualquer guitarra que possua 4 ou 5 bobinas, sendo que o número final de timbres obtido depende do número de bobinas disponíveis, ou seja, em guitarras com 2 humbuckers(HH) ou com 2 singles e um Humbucker(SSH) é possível obter 20 timbres diferentes, já em guitarras com 2 humbuckers e 1 single(HSH) é possível obter 30 timbres diferentes.

Does the Chimera System take the noise out of the guitar?

Embora não tenha sido projetado com esse objetivo, devido à excelente blindagem da placa, se a instalação for bem feita, o nível de ruído é bem baixo, mesmo com captadores single, permitindo o uso de distorção mesmo com captadores single e tornando o sistema ideal para gravações.

Is the Chimera System passive or active?

From the point of view of the need for energy for the system to function, the Chimera System is active, as it needs an energy source to function.

However, from the point of view of the connections made between the pickups, we can say that the system is passive, as it does not change the sound of the pickups at all, since there is no amplification, equalization or compensation circuit between the pickup and the guitar jack, just pure sound coming from the pickups, and it's even possible to measure the resistance of the pickups with a multimeter through the guitar jack, just like on a guitar without the system.

Does the Chimera System need a battery to work?

The Chimera System needs a power source whose voltage can vary from 3V to 5V. However, this energy can be supplied to the system in several ways. Through the battery free kit (optional), which consists of a cable and a 5V power supply. This cable conducts at the same time the energy necessary to feed the system and the guitar signal to the amplifier, or through batteries of various types, as long as they supply a voltage within the specified range. The list below provides some battery combination options that can be used:

– One model CR2032 (3V) button-type battery;

– Two standard or alkaline (3V) toothpick batteries (AAA);

– Two small (AA) normal or alkaline (3V) batteries;

– Three standard or alkaline (4.5V) toothpick batteries (AAA);

– Three small (AA) normal or alkaline (4.5V) batteries;

– Three rechargeable toothpick (AAA) batteries (3.6V);

– Three small (AA) rechargeable batteries (3.6V);

– Four rechargeable toothpick(AAA) batteries (4.8V);

– Four small (AA) rechargeable batteries (4.8V);

What is the difference between the Chimera System and the Variax from line 6?

The Chimera System is an analog switching system, which expands your guitar's sonic possibilities, but preserves the natural sound and dynamics of your pickups, and can be installed on any guitar.

Line 6's Variax is a digital system that tries to reproduce the tone of traditional guitars and comes pre-installed on guitars manufactured by Line 6.

What is the difference between the Chimera System and the switching system that comes installed in the new high-end guitars from Gibson?

The Chimera System is an analog switching system, which expands your guitar's sonic possibilities, but preserves the natural sound and dynamics of your pickups, and can be installed on any guitar.

In addition, it allows complex connections that are not possible with common switches or push-pull potentiometers, but its interface is very simple, with only a 3-position mini-switch and a push-pull potentiometer, for selection of up to 6 timbre banks.

Gibson's switching system has 4 push-pull potentiometers, plus a set of dip-switches on the board that sits inside the guitar, which makes the interface quite confusing.

But despite that, it only allows simple connections, like split pickups and parallel connections between some coils.

Installing the Chimera System on my guitar, will I lose its original tones?

No. Your guitar's original tones remain unchanged.

Is the installation of the Chimera System complicated?

No. System installation is very easy.

Anyone who knows how to use a soldering iron can install the system, as all terminals on the board are identified and when you buy the Chimera System you receive a manual with all the instructions for installing any type of guitar.
